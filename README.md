# Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation
Applies forecasting, simulation, and optimisation techniques to improve operational efficiency in a service-based company. Covers demand forecasting, inventory control, job allocation, and queuing models to minimise costs, reduce delays, and optimise resource utilisation.
## Project Overview
This project focuses on applying operations and supply chain analytics to improve decision making and efficiency within a service-based organisation. Using historical demand data, the analysis explores patterns in customer subscription calls and identifies seasonality and trends through time series techniques.

Multiple forecasting models, including seasonal naïve, seasonal average, and seasonal exponential smoothing, are evaluated to predict future demand. Among these, exponential smoothing demonstrates the highest accuracy, supporting more reliable planning and resource allocation.

The project further incorporates inventory simulation using a continuous review system to analyse ordering, holding, and backorder costs. Insights highlight the trade-offs between inventory levels and service efficiency, with recommendations to optimise reorder points and maintain safety stock.

To enhance operational efficiency, linear programming is applied to engineer job allocation, minimising travel distance and improving cost effectiveness. Additionally, queuing theory and Poisson-based simulation are used to evaluate vehicle servicing operations, identifying inefficiencies in mechanic utilisation and customer wait times.

Overall, the project demonstrates how integrating forecasting, optimisation, and simulation models can significantly improve operational performance, reduce costs, and enhance customer satisfaction in a dynamic business environment.

## 3.	Forecasting New Demand 
For any organization to plan and strategize resource allocation and marketing efforts, forecasting is essential. Three forecast models were applied to analyze the calls data for Delta Sky Broadcasting: Seasonal Naïve, Seasonal Average and Seasonal Exponential Smoothing. Seasonal Naïve model assumes that the demand for each day in a week will be the same as the corresponding day of the previous week. Seasonal Average takes a mean of the previous observations and eliminates any random fluctuations. Seasonal Exponential Smoothing makes use of alpha and gamma to train the model to adapt to recent changes in the data.

![4](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Seasonal%20Naive%20Forecasting.png)

The Seasonal model displays a similar forecast to the actual sales data but with a higher peak on the 22nd of June. For in-sample performance, the ME (Mean Error) is a positive value of 143 with MAE (Mean Absolute Error) being 295.17 entailing that forecasts deviate from the actual data by this number of calls per day. The RMSE (Root Mean Square Error) is 432, higher than MAE, which validates the accuracy of the model. For out of sample MAE is higher, 362, which indicates slight inaccuracy when predicting new data.

![5](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Seasonal%20Average%20Forecasting.png)

The Seasonal Average Model shows much lower peaks than the actual data, smoothing out the upwards trend. The in-sample ME is higher than the previous model, of 262 calls meaning it is slightly more inaccurate. For out of sample performance, it has substantially larger errors than the Seasonal Naïve model, with ME being 597 and RMSE being 676 calls. While this model provides smoothing and stability, it lacks sensitivity and adaptability to change. 

![6](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Seasonal%20Exponential%20Forecasting.png)

The in-sample performance for the Seasonal Exponential model has the lowest bias, with ME as 34 calls. The RMSE is slightly higher than the MAE, having a value of 238, indicating that the model is relatively consistent. The out of sample performances also display lower biasness and errors than the previous models. The ME is 21 calls, MAE 211 calls and RMSE holding a value of 329. Thus, the Seasonal exponential smoothing model remains the most accurate in its prediction and forecasting.  
## Inventory Simulation
Delta Sky Broadcasting makes use of a continuous review inventory system. This system makes continuous reviews of the stock on hand and reorder point. The reorder point is 2 boxes, and the reorder quantity is 10 boxes. The demand is determined by the number of installations and the boxes required for each one. While this method is highly responsive in case there are fluctuations in demand, ordering costs might be high. The random number generator in the data analysis tool pack had been used to simulate daily installations and boxes required. 

![7](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Inventory%20Forecasting%20Tables.png)

Installations are calculated using the cut-off value, from the cumulative probability. The random numbers are then assessed to see which cut off number their align with, that corresponds to the respective number of installations. Inventory is updated daily with negative ending inventory, for example on day 2 meaning a backorder of 2 boxes. Orders are placed when there are less than or equal to 2 boxes in the inventory. The reorder quantity is always 10. Hence, on day 6, when there was a deficit of 6 boxes, 10 new ones were ordered, which then totaled to be the beginning inventory of day 7 after addressing the back orders. This also triggers a back-order penalty cost of 20. The costs incurred are essential for any organization. 

The highest costs were incurred on day 6, which can be an indication of demand for future forecasting and planning. For example, if the company knows that they would have the most installation demand on week 5, prior planning can prevent scarcity of stock. This cost structure highlights the tradeoff between the costs. Frequent orders cause more ordering costs but might result in exemption from back-order costs as there would be no deficit of boxes in the inventory.  

Higher costs means that the company might want to try another inventory method. The ordering cost is always 500, which is the amount to order new boxes. The holding cost is the monetary value of storing the box in inventory for a day. This includes the storage and expenses related to that. Lastly, the backorder cost is a penalty cost for delayed services, in the form of financial and reputation impact to Delta Sky. For one week, the total cost incurred by the company is 1,930 with an average daily cost of 275.71. In total, new stock was ordered 3 times while backorder cost was triggered twice.  

Overall, implementing more accurate demand forecasting methods can help inventory planning. It is also helpful to consider holding a safety stock. Safety stock acts as a safety net, so even in times of high demand, there won’t be a shortage of boxes. This can also diminish backorder costs. Order lead time should also be negotiated with suppliers so that Delta TV does not incur large back-order cost. The reorder quantity should also be increased, as 2 boxes are on the lower side. By increasing the reorder point and order quantity, the organization will have more stock in hand to deal with demand fluctuations. Shortage of stocks can not only cause stress to the company but also a loss of confidence and increased agitation from the customer.  While the continuous inventory system is effective, adjustments to order quantity and costs would help Delta TV incur less expenses.
## Engineer Job Allocation
As previously mentioned, resource allocation allows companies to plan efficiently to coordinate internal organizational operations. Employees are one of the main and important resources for a service-oriented company. This includes efficient allocation of installation jobs for engineers. The current manual allocation process that uses employee availability and manual judgement might not reap the best results. To address this issue and optimize the process, linear-programming is applied to minimize the distance required to travel by the engineers, reducing costs and time consumption. 5 engineers, Tom, Bob, Paul, Steve and Lucas are expected to work on 5 installation jobs. 

![8](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Distance%20Matrix.png)

The distance matrix shows the distance, in miles, that each engineer needs to travel for each job. For example, Tom would travel 14 miles for job 1. This provides insights into the costs incurred by each employee travelling that distance and as a result, their efficiency.

![9](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Decision%20Matrix.png)

The decision matrix displays a binary matrix. Each job can only be done by one engineer, however there is no limit on the number of jobs one engineer can perform. The job allocations are done using linear programming for optimal assignment, Steve is assigned to Job 1 as he has to cover the least distance for it (8 miles). Similarly, Paul is assigned to Job 2 and 4, Tom to Job 3 and Lucas to Job 5. This promotes cost efficiency.

![10](https://github.com/nehasiddiqui0026-tech/Data-Driven-Operations-and-Supply-Chain-Analytics-for-Demand-Forecasting-and-Resource-Optimisation/blob/main/tables%20and%20visualisations/Optimal%20Distance%20Matrix.png)

The total distance matrix is an output of the multiplication of the Distance and Decision matrix. The cells which are empty signify no correspondence between the employee and the specific job. This matrix provides an overview of the total distances travelled for all assignments or jobs. Job 1 would be assigned to Steve, who must travel 8 miles. Job 2 is assigned to Paul which is only at 3 miles. Similarly, Tom is responsible for job 3, with a similar distance. Job 4 is assigned again to Paul, who now has a cumulate travel distance of 8 miles. Lastly, Job 5 is tasked to Lucas, who must cover 8 miles. The total distance for all engineers is 27 miles.  

Linear programming provides more operational efficiency for Delta Sky Broadcasting. It reduces the operational costs that would be incurred if an engineer was assigned to a job at a longer distance than another engineer. For example, bob would have to travel 30 miles for Job 5, which only requires Lucas to travel 8 miles for. Cost cutting is a crucial way companies can handle their financial position. Optimal resource allocation ensures that each job is handled by an employee that is positioned at a geographically efficient distance. This reduces overtime and also minimizes salaries. This removes human bias and allows for more fairness and consistent job allocations.  

Delta Sky Broadcasting can benefit from regular implementation of linear programming to assign jobs to employees. This consistent cost-saving method would also allow for operational improvements. This can also be integrated into the company’s scheduling software, so each engineer is automatically made aware of the jobs and roles they are assigned to avoid any conflicts and biasness. This also reduces many efforts and removes inaccuracies. This model can be improved by taking into consideration feedback from the engineers that are assigned to deduce whether they were content and satisfied with the assignments.  

Reviewing the outcomes of the model also provides an opportunity to revise and improve it for further accuracy. Using mathematical optimization for job allocation is a powerful tool to reduce costs and improve service efficiency. It removes the occurrence of bias when tasking an employee with a role and improves employee morale. For a service-based company, Delta Sky Broadcasting should prioritize the satisfaction of their most important resource, their employees. This will boost the organizational customer and lead to more efficient performance. As a result, company revenues would increase along with customer satisfaction.
## Vehicle Servicing and Maintenance  
Maintenance is imperative for the fulfilment of optimal performance. Delta Sky Broadcasting’s fleet ensures successful installation by engineers. The company operates a service hub equipped with two mechanics that service vehicles. This process follows a Poisson process with a mean of 20 minutes per vehicle. The Poisson distribution is calculated using the time between intervals. When each vehicle arrives, it is then assigned to a mechanic that is available first with the least current completion time. This reduces waiting time for the customer and ensures satisfaction.   

The model is designed to count the occurrence of an event in queuing systems using random numerical generator in the data analysis pack, with seed is 2866. The start time for the vehicle is the maximum of its arrival time and the service time is extracted from an exponential distribution with an average of 20 minutes. The idle time and wait time are also calculated for the gap between the completion of one service and the start of another. The weight time is calculated using the start and completion time for each mechanic.

The simulation results display the vehicles along with the time between arrival and arrival time. The number of mechanics working on each vehicle are also shown for more clarity. Mechanic one has a total idle time of 108 minutes with an idle time percentage of 36.85 percent. The randomness of the services and the allocation of the vehicles define these values.

Mechanic 2 displays a total idle time of 131 minutes which is more than mechanic one, and an idle time percentage of 55 percent. Mechanic two works in 8 vehicles while mechanic 1 works on 7, which can explain the disparity int the result times. This may signify an imbalance of workload distribution. The maximum weight time is 16 minutes for vehicle 12. Delta Sky Broadcasting should continue to track the service patterns of the mechanics to monitor customer satisfaction. To reduce idle time, allocation rules should be implemented such as pre-bookings and appointments. This gives a clear image to the mechanic of the time they can expect the vehicle, and the customer does not have to wait either.  

While it might be more costly for the company to hire more mechanics to divide the workload, it provides the customers with better experience. Investing in more resources can lead to long-term benefits, such as customer loyalty, brand reputation and new clients. Leveraging the simulation and queuing theory can provide insights for Delta Sky about their existing operations and workforce.
